MicroChange

Every year, 8 million tonnes of plastic ends up in the ocean.

This issue harms marine life and even so it still goes ignored.

However, this addition in the diet of sea life affects us as well.

Without noticing, these small plastic particles we consume are what is called 'microplastic'.

Microplastics are considered this when plastic particles are smaller than 5mm. Ultimately, microplastics can be found in a variety of settings, including the oceans, rivers, lakes, soil, air, and even some food sources. They represent substantial environmental and possibly health problems as microplastic can survive in the environment for long periods of time, are consumed by wildlife, and eventually reach the human food chain.

We can help prevent this by cleaning up our local beaches and environments and join groups to help tackle the problem on a larger scale!

Big thanks to the those taking part to make the world a cleaner place!
